The History of Halloween

Origins of Halloween

Halloween originated with the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain. The Celts celebrated this on October 31st to mark the end of the harvest and the beginning of winter, a season they associated with death. They believed that on this night, the boundary between the living and the dead blurred, allowing spirits to roam the earth.

How Halloween Evolved

Over time, Halloween transformed as Christianity spread. The day became known as All Hallows' Eve, preceding All Saints' Day. Immigrants brought Halloween traditions to North America, where it evolved to include activities like trick-or-treating, pumpkin carving, and wearing costumes.
